Who am I

My name is Liu Honghui (刘鸿辉）. Currently, I’m a Ph.D student of the Department of Physics, Fudan University. I’m particularly interested in studying X-ray radiation from the circumnuclear environment of black holes, either for stellar mass black holes in X-ray binaries or supermassive black holes in the center of most galaxies. The X-ray spectra and time series from the vicinity of accreting black holes carry rich signatures of the spacetime and the accretion/ejection material near black holes.


Testing gravity theory

When the accretion disk extends down to the innermost stable circular orbit (ISCO), radiation from the inner disk inevitably has imprint of the spacetime near the black hole. Thus, analyzing the spectra of black hole accretion disks is naturally a good technique to test gravity theory (see Bambi et al, 2017). I’m experienced at using the X-ray spectra to constrain possible deviation from the Kerr metric of the spacetime near black holes.
