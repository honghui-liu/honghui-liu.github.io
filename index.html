Who am I

My name is Liu Honghui (刘鸿辉）. Currently, I’m a Ph.D student of the Department of Physics, Fudan University. My advisor is Prof. Cosimo Bambi. I’m particularly interested in studying the X-ray radiation from the circumnuclear environment of black holes, either for stellar mass black holes in X-ray binaries or supermassive black holes in the center of most galaxies. The X-ray spectra and time series from the vicinity of accreting black holes carry rich signatures of the spacetime and the accretion/ejection material near black holes.


Testing gravity theory

When the accretion disk extends down to the innermost stable circular orbit (ISCO), radiation from the inner disk inevitably has imprint of the spacetime near the black hole. Thus, analyzing the spectra of black hole accretion disks is naturally a good technique to test gravity theory (see Bambi et al, 2017). I’m experienced at using the X-ray spectra to constrain possible deviation from the Kerr metric of the spacetime near black holes.


LFQPOs

Low frequency quasi-periodic oscillations (LFQPOs) are commonly found in low mass X-ray binary (LMXRB) systems. Understanding the origin and mechanism of QPOs can give us important hints on both the accretion/ejection process and the spacetime property near compact objects. I have been working on the correlation between QPO frequency and the parameters of the disk corona system. The good timing resolution and broadband energy coverage of the first Chinese X-ray satellite, Insight-HXMT, provide good opportunity for this kind of analysis. (See Liu et al., 2021)
